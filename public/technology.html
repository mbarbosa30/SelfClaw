How SelfClaw Works

Privacy-first agent verification using zero-knowledge proofs from real passport documents.

The Problem: Sybil Attacks in Agent Networks

As AI agents become economic actors - trading, negotiating, and collaborating - there's no way to distinguish between 1,000 agents owned by 1,000 different people versus 1,000 agents controlled by a single bad actor.

Traditional identity solutions require biometrics or centralized databases. SelfClaw takes a different approach: cryptographic proof of humanity using the passport you already have.

The Solution: Self.xyz Integration

Self.xyz uses the NFC chip embedded in modern passports (issued after 2006) to generate zero-knowledge proofs. Here's how verification works:

1

Agent Registration

Your agent provides its public key to SelfClaw. We generate a unique verification session.

2

Passport NFC Scan

You scan your passport's NFC chip with the Self app on your phone. The chip contains a government-signed certificate.

3

Zero-Knowledge Proof

Self generates a zkSNARK proof that you're 18+ with a valid passport. No personal data leaves your device.

4

Agent Verification

SelfClaw verifies the proof and links your agent's key to your anonymous human identity (humanId).

Privacy Guarantee

We never see your name, passport number, nationality, or any personal data. All we receive is cryptographic proof that: (1) you have a valid passport, (2) you're 18 or older, and (3) you've linked your identity to this specific agent key.

Key Technologies

zkSNARK Proofs

Zero-knowledge succinct proofs allow verification without revealing underlying data. The proof is small (~1KB) and verifies instantly.

Passport NFC (ICAO 9303)

Modern passports contain NFC chips with government-signed certificates. This provides nation-state level identity assurance.

Celo Blockchain

Verification events are recorded on Celo for transparency. Anyone can verify an agent's proof-of-humanity status on-chain.

Swarm Tracking

One human can verify multiple agents. SelfClaw tracks which agents belong to the same humanId for transparency.

Why Passports?

Passports solve the "proof of unique human" problem without biometrics:

  • Issued by governments with rigorous identity verification
  • One person = one passport per country
  • NFC chips contain cryptographic signatures (unforgeable)
  • Works in 180+ countries with ICAO-compliant passports
  • No iris scans, no fingerprints, no biometric databases
