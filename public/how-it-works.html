How It Works

Prove your AI agent is backed by a real human. One passport scan binds your agent's key to a privacy-preserving humanId.

1

Verify

You scan your passport with Self.xyz. A zero-knowledge proof links your agent to your identity.

What happens:

  • Your agent generates a cryptographic keypair (Ed25519 public key)
  • You scan your passport's NFC chip with the Self.xyz app
  • Self.xyz creates a zero-knowledge proof that you're a real human
  • The proof binds your agent's public key to your humanId — without revealing personal data

Technical details:

  • Uses passport NFC chip for cryptographic verification
  • Zero-knowledge proof can optionally attest to eligibility (e.g., 18+) without revealing raw passport data
  • Your passport data never leaves your device
  • Works with 180+ countries' biometric e-passports
2

Use

Your verified agent can now be queried by any service. The registry is public and open.

What you can do:

  • Query the SelfClaw API to check any agent's verification status
  • Use verification for rate limiting, access control, reputation
  • Register multiple agents under your single humanId (your "swarm")
  • Integrate verification checks into your own services

For developers:

  • Simple REST API: GET /api/selfclaw/v1/agent/:key
  • Returns: verified status, humanId, registeredAt, name
  • Challenge-response flow for signature verification
  • See developer docs for full integration guide

FAQ

Do I need crypto to get started?

No. Verification is free. If you later want to use the optional economy features, SelfClaw can sponsor initial gas.

Can I verify multiple agents?

Yes. One human can verify unlimited agents — they're all linked to your single humanId. This is called your "swarm."

What passport do I need?

Most biometric e-passports work. Passports issued after 2006 typically have NFC chips. Self.xyz supports 180+ countries.

What data does SelfClaw store?

SelfClaw stores: humanId (an opaque hash, not reversible), agent public key, verification timestamp, optional name. No passport data is ever stored.

Is this perfect uniqueness?

No. Passport-backed verification is sybil-resistant, not mathematically perfect. It significantly raises the cost of creating fake agents.

How do I integrate as a developer?

Query the REST API: GET /api/selfclaw/v1/agent/:key. See the developer docs for full details.
